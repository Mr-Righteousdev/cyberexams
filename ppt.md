**SLIDE 1 — Title Slide**
_Job: First impression. Look serious before you say a word._

- Subtitle: A Case Study of Verona High School, Mutundwe — Uganda

- Design: Dark navy full background, white text, thin accent line under the title. Clean. No clutter.

---

**SLIDE 2 — The Scene (Context)**
_Job: Set up the world this problem lives in. One paragraph, max 3 bullets._

- Uganda introduced CBC in lower secondary schools in 2020
- CBC demands continuous tracking of 7 core competencies per student
- Schools have no technology to do this — teachers track everything manually with class sizes of 70+
- Visual idea: A simple split — left side says "What CBC Demands", right side says "What Schools Have". Stark contrast.

---

**SLIDE 3 — The Problem (The Pain)**
_Job: Make the room feel the problem. This is your emotional anchor slide._

- One bold statement: **"A student can silently fail an entire competency — and no one notices until it's too late."**
- 3 consequences below:
    - Students fall behind with zero targeted support
    - Teachers can't individually monitor 70+ learners
    - School has no data-driven picture of competency development
- Design: This slide should feel slightly heavier — maybe a subtle red accent or dark background just on this one to signal urgency

---

**SLIDE 4 — The Evidence (Why This Is Real)**
_Job: Hit them with numbers before they question the problem._

- 3 statistics, large and bold:
    - **70%+** of CBC teachers in Uganda never attended training _(Kidega et al., 2024)_
    - **491 learners** surveyed — CBC assessment still memory-based, not competency-based _(Atibuni et al., 2024)_
    - **22,304 students** — AI competency prediction accuracy of **0.9997** _(ScienceDirect, 2025)_
- Design: Three big number cards side by side. Numbers huge, source small underneath.

---

**SLIDE 5 — The Research Gap**
_Job: Show nobody has solved this yet. This is where you own the space._

- Global systems exist: Knewton, DreamBox, LMS dashboards
- But: Built for Western, well-resourced, higher education environments
- In Uganda: No documented intelligent system for CBC competency gap monitoring exists
- One line at the bottom, bold: **"This study will build the first."**

---

**SLIDE 6 — Proposed Solution (What You're Building)**
_Job: The reveal. One clear system description, no jargon overload._

- System name/title prominent
- 4-step flow visual (simple horizontal arrows):
    - **INPUT** → Teacher enters assessment data
    - **ANALYSE** → System maps data against CBC competency benchmarks
    - **DETECT** → Gap detection engine flags declining competency patterns
    - **ALERT** → Teacher receives specific intervention recommendation
- One line: _"Not a marking system. An intelligence layer that watches, notices, and acts."_

---

**SLIDE 7 — What The System Does (Features)**
_Job: Make it concrete. What does the teacher actually see?_

- 4 modules, brief:
    - 📊 Competency Tracking Dashboard — student and class level
    - 🚨 Automated Gap Alerts — specific competency, specific student
    - 💡 Intervention Recommendations — actionable, not vague
    - 📈 Historical Trend Visualisation — competency growth over time
- Design: Icon + label + one-line description for each. Clean grid layout.

---

**SLIDE 8 — Theoretical Foundation**
_Job: Show you read. One slide, not a lecture._

- 3 frameworks, brief:
    - **Bloom's Taxonomy** — structure for measuring competency levels
    - **Formative Assessment Cycle** _(Black & Wiliam, 1998)_ — gap detection triggers iterative teaching response
    - **Theory of Early Intervention** — earlier detection = lower cost to learner
- Design: Three boxes, one per framework. Clean, not crowded.

---

**SLIDE 9 — Methodology Snapshot**
_Job: Show you have a plan. Not the full chapter — just enough._

- Case Study: Verona High School, Mutundwe, Kampala
- Approach: Mixed methods — interviews + questionnaires + document review
- Sample: 10 CBC teachers, 2 administrators, 60 S1/S2 students
- Stack: Laravel · Livewire · MySQL · Tailwind CSS
- Timeline: January 2025 – November 2025
- Design: Two columns — left for research side, right for technical side

---

**SLIDE 10 — Significance & Close**
_Job: Land the plane. Leave them with the "so what" burned in their minds._
